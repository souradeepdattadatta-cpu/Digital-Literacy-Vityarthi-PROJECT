# Case Study: Phishing Attack (Real-World Example)

## Introduction  
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations such as banks, delivery services, or government agencies to steal sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, and banking details. It is one of the most common and dangerous online threats faced by internet users today.

## Real Incident  
In India, several users reported receiving fraudulent SMS messages claiming to be from banks like SBI or services like India Post. These messages asked users to urgently update their KYC details or track a parcel. The message included a link that redirected users to a fake website designed to look exactly like the official one. Many users unknowingly entered their personal and banking details, leading to unauthorized transactions and financial losses.

## How the Attack Works (Step-by-Step)  
1. The attacker sends a fake SMS or email posing as a trusted organization.  
2. The message creates urgency (e.g., account suspension or delivery failure).  
3. A malicious link is provided to a fake but realistic-looking website.  
4. The victim enters login credentials, bank details, or OTP.  
5. The attacker collects this data and uses it to access accounts and transfer money.  

## Target Audience  
Phishing attacks mainly target students, online shoppers, and working professionals who frequently use digital services but may not verify the authenticity of messages or links.

## Consequences  
Victims may face financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data. In some cases, recovering stolen money is difficult, and it can also cause stress and loss of trust in digital platforms.

## Conclusion  
This case study highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using online services. Users should always verify links, avoid sharing sensitive information, and report suspicious activities to prevent such cybercrimes.
